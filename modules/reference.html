📋 P-T Chart Reference

Keep R-134a P-T chart in field kit. Use for quick pressure-to-temperature conversions. Critical for diagnosing system performance.

Normal Operating Baseline
ParameterNormal Value

Gauge Pattern Guide
ConditionLow SideHigh SideDuct TempClue
Normal / Good System 25–45 psi 150–250 psi 45–53°F Baseline. Memorize this.
Low Charge ↓ Low ↓ Low Warm Both sides low. Clutch may not engage (pressure switch cutout).
Overcharge ↑ High ↑↑ Very High Warm Both sides elevated. High side excessively high.
Blocked Condenser ↑ High ↑↑ Very High Warm Same pattern as overcharge — check airflow first!
Blocked Evap / Cab Filters ↓ Low ↓ Low Cold (no airflow) System works, no airflow to cab. Blower check too.
Moisture in System Fluctuates Fluctuates Intermittent TXV freezing and thawing. Erratic cycling.
Component Specifications
⚙️
Compressor

Clutch engagement voltage: 12V. Oil capacity: varies by model. Belt tension: 1/4" deflection. Check service manual for specific specs.

🌡️
Condenser

Airflow requirement: minimum 400 CFM. Fin spacing: standard. Clean monthly in dusty conditions. Check for damage.

🫙
Receiver/Dryer

Relief valve: 535 psi. Replace when system opened. Desiccant capacity: finite. Check service manual for replacement intervals.

❄️
Evaporator

Freeze protection: EDT cycles compressor. Drain must be clear. Duct temp: 45-53°F normal. Below 40°F possible freeze-up.

Tools Required

Gauge set, recovery machine, vacuum pump, refrigerant scale, UV light, leak detector, thermometer, torque wrenches.

📋
Documentation

Record concern, cause, correction. Log refrigerant amounts. Document oil recovered and added. Keep recovery records.
